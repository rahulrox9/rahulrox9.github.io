भागः १: प्रस्तावना (Introduction)

संस्कृतम् एकम् अत्यन्तं शुद्धम् ध्वन्यात्मकं च भाषारूपम् अस्ति । Sanskrit is a highly precise and phonetic language.

In Sanskrit, sound is fundamental. Each letter represents a distinct sound, and the sounds themselves are arranged systematically. For this reason, Sanskrit is best approached not merely as a written language, but as a language of carefully ordered sound.

This first lesson introduces the basic sound structure of Sanskrit: the vowels (svarāḥ), the consonants (vyañjanāni), and the alphabet as an organised phonetic system. It also introduces IAST, a standard method of accurately transliterating Sanskrit using the Latin alphabet.

भागः २: वर्णमाला (The Sanskrit Alphabet)

The Sanskrit alphabet is called the varṇamālā (वर्णमाला), literally a “garland of letters.” It is not arranged arbitrarily. Rather, the letters are organised according to how and where each sound is produced in the mouth.

Key idea: Sanskrit letters are organised by place of articulation and manner of articulation. This is one of the most remarkable features of the language.

स्वराः (Vowels)

Vowels are sounds that can be pronounced independently. In Sanskrit they are called svarāḥ (स्वराः).

Type Devanāgarī IAST
Short vowels अ इ उ ऋ ऌ a, i, u, ṛ, ḷ
Long vowels आ ई ऊ ॠ ā, ī, ū, ṝ
Diphthongs ए ऐ ओ औ e, ai, o, au

The vowels may also be classified as hṛasva (short), dīrgha (long), and in some contexts pluta (prolated). At this stage, the important distinction is simply that vowel length matters. For example, a and ā are not the same sound.

व्यञ्जनानि (Consonants)

Consonants are called vyañjanāni (व्यञ्जनानि). They are arranged in groups based on where they are pronounced.

Group Place of articulation Letters IAST
क-वर्गः Guttural (throat) क ख ग घ ङ ka kha ga gha ṅa
च-वर्गः Palatal (palate) च छ ज झ ञ ca cha ja jha ña
ट-वर्गः Retroflex (cerebral) ट ठ ड ढ ण ṭa ṭha ḍa ḍha ṇa
त-वर्गः Dental (teeth) त थ द ध न ta tha da dha na
प-वर्गः Labial (lips) प फ ब भ म pa pha ba bha ma

Within each consonant group, the order is fixed. The sounds proceed as follows:

  1. unvoiced unaspirated
  2. unvoiced aspirated
  3. voiced unaspirated
  4. voiced aspirated
  5. nasal

Thus, the Sanskrit alphabet is both phonetic and analytical. It teaches the learner how sounds are produced, not merely how they are written.

भागः ३: अन्तःस्थाः, ऊष्माणः, अयोगवाहाः

अन्तःस्थाः (Semi-vowels)

These occupy an intermediate position between vowels and consonants:

Devanāgarī IAST
य र ल वya ra la va

ऊष्माणः (Sibilants and aspirate)

These are the breath-based or fricative sounds:

Devanāgarī IAST Note
शśapalatal ś
षṣaretroflex ṣ
सsadental s
हhaaspirate

अयोगवाहाः (Auxiliary signs)

These are not independent letters in the same sense, but they are essential in Sanskrit pronunciation and writing.

Sign Name IAST Function
ं अनुस्वारः ṃ nasal resonance
ः विसर्गः ḥ breath release after a vowel
ँ अनुनासिकः / चन्द्रबिन्दुः ̃ nasalisation

भागः ४: IAST (Transliteration)

Since Sanskrit is traditionally written in Devanāgarī, a precise method is needed to represent the same sounds in the Latin alphabet. This method is called IAST, the International Alphabet of Sanskrit Transliteration.

IAST is important because ordinary English spelling cannot capture Sanskrit accurately. For example, ṭ and t are different sounds, just as ś, ṣ, and s are different sounds.

Devanāgarī IAST Why it matters
तtadental sound
टṭaretroflex sound
शśapalatal sibilant
षṣaretroflex sibilant
सsadental sibilant
कृष्णःkṛṣṇaḥshows ṛ, ṣ, and ḥ clearly

Practical rule: throughout these lessons, Sanskrit words may be given in Devanāgarī, in IAST, or in both. Learners should become comfortable with both scripts gradually.

भागः ५: उदाहरणानि (Examples)

स्वराः

  • अग्निः — agniḥ
  • आत्मा — ātmā
  • ईश्वरः — īśvaraḥ
  • ऋषिः — ṛṣiḥ

व्यञ्जनानि

  • कवि — kavi
  • गुरुः — guruḥ
  • तपः — tapaḥ
  • भक्तिः — bhaktiḥ

At this stage, the aim is not memorisation alone, but recognition. The learner should begin to notice the distinction between short and long vowels, between dental and retroflex sounds, and between Devanāgarī and IAST representation.

भागः ७: अभ्यासः (Exercises)

प्रश्नाः (Questions)

  1. Name the five main consonant groups.
  2. What is the difference between a short vowel and a long vowel?
  3. What is IAST, and why is it useful?
  4. Identify whether the following are vowels or consonants: अ, उ, क, त, श.
  5. Write the IAST forms of: कृष्णः, अग्निः, गुरुः, आत्मा.

उत्तराणि (Answers)

  1. क-वर्गः, च-वर्गः, ट-वर्गः, त-वर्गः, प-वर्गः
  2. A short vowel is brief in duration; a long vowel is extended in duration.
  3. IAST is the International Alphabet of Sanskrit Transliteration. It represents Sanskrit sounds accurately in the Latin script.
  4. अ and उ are vowels; क, त, and श are consonants.
  5. kṛṣṇaḥ, agniḥ, guruḥ, ātmā
