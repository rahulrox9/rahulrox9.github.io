Basic Lesson 01:
Sounds, Vowels, and Consonants

How to read this lesson:

Each word appears in three forms:

• Simple pronunciation
• IAST (precise transliteration)
• Sanskrit script

Example:
krishna → kṛṣṇa → कृष्ण

Part 1: Introduction

Sanskrit is a precise and phonetic language. Every letter represents a distinct sound.

Letters are called varnaaha (IAST: varṇāḥ; Sanskrit: वर्णाः).

Vowels are svaraaha (svarāḥ; स्वराः).

Consonants are vyanjanaani (vyañjanāni; व्यञ्जनानि).

Part 3: Consonants

Aspirated vs Unaspirated vs Nasal

Some sounds release a small burst of air. These are aspirated.

Some do not. These are unaspirated.

Some sounds go through the nose. These are nasal.
Try this: pinch your nose and say “m” — it becomes difficult.

Each consonant group follows this pattern:

unaspirated → aspirated → voiced → aspirated → nasal

Important:
Consonants are not complete sounds by themselves. They are half-sounds.

In practice, we pronounce them with an “a” added: ka, kha, ga, etc.
