Basic Lesson 01:
Sounds, Vowels, and Consonants

Part 1: Introduction

Sanskrit is a highly precise and phonetic language. Each letter represents a distinct sound, and these sounds are organised systematically.


In Sanskrit, letters or sound-units are called varnaaha (IAST: varṇāḥ; Sanskrit: वर्णाः).


Vowels are called svaraaha (IAST: svarāḥ; Sanskrit: स्वराः), and consonants are called vyanjanaani (IAST: vyañjanāni; Sanskrit: व्यञ्जनानि).


This lesson introduces the basic sound system of Sanskrit. At this stage, the goal is first recognition and familiarity and then memorisation.

Part 2: The Sanskrit Alphabet

The Sanskrit alphabet is called varnamaala (IAST: varṇamālā; Sanskrit: वर्णमाला), literally a “garland of letters.”

Key idea: Sanskrit letters are organised by how and where sounds are produced in the mouth.

Vowels

Vowels are sounds that can be pronounced independently. In Sanskrit they are called svaraaha (IAST: svarāḥ; Sanskrit: स्वराः).

Vowel length is important. For example, a and ā are different sounds.

Part 3: Additional Sounds

Semi-vowels

These are called antahstha (IAST: antaḥsthāḥ; Sanskrit: अन्तःस्थाः).

Letters Simple IAST Audio

Sibilants and aspirate

These are called ushmanaha (IAST: ūṣmāṇaḥ; Sanskrit: ऊष्माणः).

Letters Simple IAST Audio

These are called ayogavaha (IAST: ayogavāhāḥ; Sanskrit: अयोगवाहाः).

Sign Description IAST Audio

Part 4: IAST (Transliteration)

Up to this point, simplified spellings have been used for ease of reading. Sanskrit, however, is represented more accurately using IAST (International Alphabet of Sanskrit Transliteration).

IAST distinguishes sounds that English cannot represent clearly.

Devanāgarī IAST Meaning
तtadental
टṭaretroflex
शśapalatal
षṣaretroflex

Important: IAST will be used more frequently in later lessons.

Part 5: Examples

  • agniḥ (अग्निः)
  • ātmā (आत्मा)
  • guruḥ (गुरुः)
  • kṛṣṇaḥ (कृष्णः)

The goal is to recognise patterns in sound and script.
