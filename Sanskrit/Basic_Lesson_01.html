Basic Lesson 01:
Sounds, Vowels, and Consonants

How to read this lesson:
Each word appears in three forms: (i) simple pronunciation, (ii) IAST, (iii) Sanskrit script.
Example: krishna → kṛṣṇa → कृष्ण

About IAST:
IAST (International Alphabet of Sanskrit Transliteration) is a system used to represent Sanskrit sounds using the Latin alphabet. English spelling is not precise enough to capture all Sanskrit sounds.

For example: ट (ṭa) vs त (ta) or श (śa) vs ष (ṣa) vs स (sa) are all different sounds in Sanskrit but would look similar in English. IAST solves this by using marks such as dots and lines to show the exact sound.

You do not need to memorise IAST yet. It is used here only to show accurate pronunciation.

Part 1: Introduction

Sanskrit is a phonetic language. Each letter represents a precise sound.

Letters are called varnaaha (varṇāḥ; वर्णाः).

Vowels are svaraaha (svarāḥ; स्वराः).

Consonants are vyanjanaani (vyañjanāni; व्यञ्जनानि).

Each consonant group follows a pattern:

  • 1st and 3rd sounds are unaspirated
  • 2nd and 4th sounds are aspirated
  • 5th sound is nasal

Note on pronunciation:
Strictly speaking, consonants in Sanskrit are not complete sounds by themselves. They are half-sounds. In practice, they are pronounced with an “a” sound added. For this reason, we say ka, kha, ga, rather than isolated consonants. This convention will be followed throughout these lessons.
