Basic Lesson 01:
Sounds, Vowels, and Consonants

Note on spelling:
In this lesson, Sanskrit words are shown in three ways:

• Simple pronunciation (for easy reading)
• IAST (a precise system using Latin letters)
• Devanāgarī (the original script)

For example:
krishna → kṛṣṇa → कृष्ण

You do not need to memorise IAST yet. It is used to show exact sounds.

Part 1: Introduction

Sanskrit is a highly precise and phonetic language. Each letter represents a distinct sound, and these sounds are organised systematically.


In Sanskrit, letters or sound-units are called varnaaha (IAST: varṇāḥ; Sanskrit: वर्णाः).


Vowels are called svaraaha (IAST: svarāḥ; Sanskrit: स्वराः), and consonants are called vyanjanaani (IAST: vyañjanāni; Sanskrit: व्यञ्जनानि).


This lesson introduces the basic sound system of Sanskrit. At this stage, the goal is first recognition and familiarity and then memorisation.

Part 2: The Sanskrit Alphabet

The Sanskrit alphabet is called varnamaalaa (IAST: varṇamālā; Sanskrit: वर्णमाला), literally a “garland of letters.”

Key idea: Sanskrit letters are organised by how and where sounds are produced in the mouth.

Vowels

Vowels are sounds that can be pronounced independently. In Sanskrit they are called svaraaha (IAST: svarāḥ; Sanskrit: स्वराः).

Vowel length is important. For example, a and ā are different sounds.
