July 15th 2017 - Nanganallur It was a calm night in Nanganallur, a southern neighbourhood in Chennai. It was so silent that one could hear the insects playing their melodies. There had been a moderate downpour throughout the day, which added to the beauty of the night. While people had opened their doors and windows to enjoy the breeze, one room had them tightly shut, with the air-conditioner running. A boy of 18 had plugged his earphones into his brand-new ASUS Zenfone and began listening to one of his favourite tracks. In any other situation, one could have heard him sing along. Yet, this wasn’t an ordinary night. He was having one of the best conversations he had ever had with a girl — that too, with the one he had a crush on. The situation was no different on the other side. Both of them had their eyes glued to their phone screens. His phone was in silent mode, and he had muted all the WhatsApp groups. WhatsApp had indeed become an integral part of the lives of several teens like him. It had been only a few days since he dared to send the first ‘Hi’ on WhatsApp. He had received her contact from a friend who was her classmate in college. ‘So, would you like to meet me for dinner sometime next week?’ he sent. The world around him froze. It was the moment of truth. The “typing…” appeared. He stared at it. It disappeared. Then appeared again. He didn’t move. Didn’t even blink. Every time it vanished, his heart sank a little. Every time it came back, hope followed. He could do nothing except wait. He switched his phone to vibrate mode, lay down, placed it on his chest, and closed his eyes. That was when he paid attention to the song. He began to sing along. The phone vibrated. He immediately got up, placed his finger on the fingerprint sensor, and unlocked it. He opened her chat to find a monosyllabic reply with an emoji: ‘Yes ;)’ She had gone offline as soon as she replied. Her last seen was visible. He couldn’t contain his excitement. That was the first time he had asked a girl out. He kept hopping, jumping, and grinning, and reached the living room where his father and sister were watching TV. “What on earth are you doing? What has gotten into you?” yelled his father. “There are people living downstairs.” “Rahul has lost his mind. He’s cuckoo bananas!” chanted his sister. As her dad rolled his eyes and glared at her, she went quiet. Meanwhile, Rahul hopped back to the bedroom. He read the conversation once again and checked if she was online. Her last seen hadn’t changed. He set the AC timer to 30 minutes and went to sleep. July 15th 2017 – Pazhavanthangal On a calm and beautiful night in Pazhavanthangal, there was one teenager who felt quite sad and lonely. None of her favourite movies, books, or music seemed to help her feel better. “What is bothering you, Sunaina?” asked her elder sister. “You’ve been gloomy all evening. You didn’t speak a word at the dinner table either.” “It’s nothing, Akka. I’m just feeling a little low,” replied Sunaina. She unlocked her phone to check for messages. There were none. “Jannu, Appa is calling you,” shouted their mother from the next room. Ananya turned to her sister. “This conversation isn’t over. We’ll continue once I’m back.” Sunaina just grunted in reply as her sister left the room. She lay down on her bed, hoping that a good night’s sleep would help, and placed her phone under the pillow. After five minutes, her phone vibrated. There was a message from a guy. ‘Hi’ She texted back, with little interest: ‘Hi. What’s up?’ ‘Nothing much. Just felt like texting you. So what’s up with you?’ ‘Oh! Text me huh? Why? Is something wrong?’ she replied. ‘Pretty much nothing. I was about to sleep actually.’ ‘No particular reason. I was bored and felt like texting someone,’ he replied. ‘Your name was on the top, so I thought I’ll text you. If you are about to sleep, I’ll text you later. Sorry for the disturbance.’ ‘No no… It’s absolutely alright. No need to be this formal, okay?’ (Grinning Face with Sweat emoji) (Rolling on the Floor Laughing emoji) The chain of texts went on for about an hour, touching various topics like books, movies, food, school inside jokes, and more. Sunaina was so engrossed in the conversation that she didn’t even notice her sister enter the room. Ananya looked at her younger sister sitting on the bed, smiling at her phone. She felt relieved that something had lifted her mood. She quietly opened her laptop and started working on her coding assignment. Out of the blue, Sunaina shrieked, startling her. “Oh my God! Oh my God! Akka, you won’t believe what just happened!” She was glowing with excitement. Ananya burst out laughing. “What is it?” “A guy has asked me out!” replied Sunaina. “Wow! That’s great, Soo. Do you like him?” “He’s such a sweet guy, you know. I thought no one noticed me in class. This has come as a big surprise.” Ananya smiled and hugged her. Both the girls then turned off the lights and went to sleep.
