Typing…

July 15, 2017 | Chennai
Nanganallur

It was a calm night in Nanganallur, a southern neighbourhood in Chennai. The rain had stopped hours ago, but the cool dampness still lingered in the air outside. Most homes had their windows open to let the breeze in. One room had everything shut, with the air-conditioner running.

An eighteen-year-old boy lay on his bed with earphones plugged into his phone. One of his favourite songs had been playing on loop, but he had barely heard it. His attention was fixed on the ongoing conversation open in front of him.

He had already typed the message.

Btw…

The reply came almost immediately.

Yeah?

He had already typed the message.

So, would you like to meet me for dinner sometime next week?

He read it once.

Then again.

His thumb hovered above the send button for a second longer than it should have.

Then he sent it.

How it all started...

The message sat there now.

The first reply did not.

Instead, the only thing that appeared on the screen was typing…

It stayed.

Then disappeared.

Then came back again.

He stared at it without blinking, trying and failing to guess what she could possibly be writing.

Pazhavanthangal

Across the city, in Pazhavanthangal, another room held a very different kind of silence.

She had been lying on her bed for a while, half-scrolling, half-staring at nothing in particular. One of those dull, heavy moods had settled over the evening. Even her phone had stopped being interesting after a point.

Then his message came.

So, would you like to meet me for dinner sometime next week?

She read it once.

Then again.

Her fingers moved before her thoughts fully caught up.

How it's going...

The first thing she typed was too sharp.

She deleted it.

The second thing she typed was too long.

She deleted that too.

Now the phone was in her hand, but the answer still wasn’t in her head.

That was when her sister stepped into the room.

“What happened?” she asked, immediately sensing something was different.

“Nothing,” she said too quickly.

Her sister stood there for another second, unconvinced, before moving further inside.

Back to Nanganallur

By then he had stopped sitting still.

He switched his phone to vibrate mode.

Lay down.

Placed the phone on his chest.

Closed his eyes.

The phone vibrated.

He sat up instantly, unlocked it, and saw an Instagram notification instead.

The disappointment was immediate.

So was the anticipation that followed it.
